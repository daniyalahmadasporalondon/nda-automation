# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Legal, operations, and founder-review users who need to process incoming NDAs quickly while still checking hard clauses carefully. They work in a repository-first workflow: intake a matter, review clause findings, confirm proposed redlines, export or send the redlined document, and retain an audit trail.

## Product Purpose

This product reviews NDAs against an Aspora playbook, uses AI-first legal assessment with deterministic comparison and validation, and presents the final review in a human-controlled workstation. Success means reviewers can trust the evidence, understand why a clause passed or failed, choose redline wording from the playbook, and send/export without hidden state or surprise changes.

## Brand Personality

Precise, restrained, and operational. The interface should feel like serious legal workflow software: dense enough for repeat review, calm enough for judgment calls, and direct about risk, evidence, and next actions.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-page styling, decorative cards, cartoonish color coding, oversized badges, redundant status pills, and UI elements that distract from the document and selected-clause analysis. Avoid burying operational controls inside help text, and avoid presenting AI output as automatic truth.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the document and selected clause as the center of gravity.
2. Separate policy editing, runtime controls, and methodology so each surface has one job.
3. Prefer explicit structured evidence over repeated summary text.
4. Make human confirmation and undo paths visible for every redline-impacting action.
5. Treat AI as the active assessor while preserving auditability and fallback clarity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use familiar product UI patterns, readable contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, clear focus states, and reduced visual noise. State should not rely on color alone; labels and structure should carry the meaning.
